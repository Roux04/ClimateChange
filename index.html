Understanding What It Is

Climate change can be described as the long-term shift
in temperature and weather patterns.
While it is meant to be a natural process, human activity
has sped up the process by an alarming amount.


The Earth's average temperature is rising at a concerning rate,
shifting in around 2 degrees Fahrenheit / 1 degree Celsius in the past 30 years. It takes
a massive amount of heat to shift the surface temperature
of the Earth, however with the help of fossil fuels, that's no longer a problem.

Global Impacts

The world has felt the effects of climate change in numerous ways.
Scroll down to learn about a few of them.

Rising Temperatures

Global temperatures have increased by approximately 1.1°C since pre-industrial times.

Sea Level Rise

Global sea levels are rising at an accelerating rate due to melting ice caps.

Biodiversity Loss

Many species are at risk of extinction due to changing habitats and temperatures.

The United Nations's Initiatives

Paris Agreement

A legally binding international treaty on climate change, adopted in 2015. The agreement aims to limit global warming to well below 2°C, preferably 1.5°C, compared to pre-industrial levels.

SDG 13: Climate Action

One of the 17 Sustainable Development Goals focusing on urgent action to combat climate change and its impacts through global cooperation and commitment.
